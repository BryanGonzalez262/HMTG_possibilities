This project is being conducted by researchers from the department of Psychological and Brain Sciences at Dartmouth College, Hanover, NH, USA. The purpose of the study is to investigate how individuals make social-decisions and social-judgments about their own preferences and beliefs, and the preferences and beliefs of others.

Your participation is voluntary. Participation involves observing, and making behavioral judgments about another person. The other person will be participating in a series of financial games, in which he will decide how to allocate money between himself and another party. Lastly you may be asked to rate this person on several social dimensions (e.g. trust, liking). These ratings will not be shown to the other participant(s). You may choose to not answer any or all questions.

The information collected will be anonymous and no identifying information will be stored with the data collected during the experiment. Identifying information will not be used in any presentation or paper written about this project and such presentations will represent the aggregation of data from groups of people.
